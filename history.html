A (very) brief overview of the history of beer:

6000
B.C.

~1900 B.C.

The oldest surviving recipe for beer is found within A Sumerian poem honoring the patron goddess of brewing, it describes the production of beer using barley.

55 B.C.

Roman legions introduce beer to Northern Europe.

1000 A.D.

Hops begins to find its way into beer, gradually increasing in popularity.

1200 A.D.

Commercial beer enterprises are by now established in England, Germany, and Austria.

1490's

Columbus found Natives in America making a beverage very similar to beer from corn and black birch sap.

1612

The first commercial brewery opened in New Amsterdam (NYC, Manhattan) after colonists advertised in London newspapers for experienced brewers.

1786

Samuel Adams operates a commercial brewery.

1842

The first golden lager is produced in Pilsen, Bohemia. Known today as Pilsner Urquell which came to be the mother of all modern lagers.

1880

Refridgeration and railroads have kickstarted a nationwide industrialization of beer in the U.S. There are now an estimated 2,300 breweries.

April 7th 1933

After 13 dry years, prohibition ends for beer in the U.S. Only 160 breweries survived.

1967

New Albion in California is the first in the rebirth of brewpubs and microbreweries in the US.

1991

The U.S. Becomes the world largest volume producer of beer. Accounting for over 20% of the global volume.

>5000 B.C.

Beer using cereal is brewed in what is today Iran.

~1600 B.C.

Egyptian texts contain 100 medical prescriptions calling for beer.

500-1000 A.D.

Brewing practices in Europe are gradually centralized to monastaries on convents.

1040

Beer is brewed for the first time at they abbey in Weihenstephan, which is today the oldest surviving brewery.

1420

German brewers develop the lager method of brewing.

1516

Bavarian brewing guilds enact the "Reinheitsgeobot" purity laws. It becomes illegal to use anything but water, barley, and hops when brewing beer (yeast was not discovered yet).

1759

Arthur Guiness begins selling porter. It is not until the 1840's that the term "Stout" becomes affiliated with Guiness.

1810

Munich establishes Oktoberfest as an official celebration.

1876

Pasteur unraveled the secrets of yeast in the fermentation process, and he also developed pasteurization to stabilize beers (22 years before the process was applied to milk!).

1900

Japans top 3 breweries today are by now well established.

1938

Elise Miller John heads Miller Brewing for 8 years as the first woman ever to run a major brewing company.

1970's

After half a century of light lager domination, the rebirth of ale begins to take place in the U.K.

October 6th 2005

Finnish brewery Laitilan introduces "Kukko Pils" as the first certified gluten-free beer.
